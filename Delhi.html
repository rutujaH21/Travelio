Introduction

Delhi is a city and national capital territory located in north-central India. It consists of two components: Old Delhi, the historic city in the north, and New Delhi, the capital of India since 1947, built in the first part of the 20th century as the capital of British India. Delhi is one of the country’s largest urban agglomerations and sits primarily on the west bank of the Yamuna River, a tributary of the Ganges River. Delhi has been the center of a succession of mighty empires and powerful kingdoms. Its numerous ruins scattered throughout the territory offer a constant reminder of its rich history. According to legend, the city was named for Raja Dhilu, a king who reigned in the region in the 1st century BCE. Delhi is also known for its beautiful architecture and landmarks.
Some of its most famous sites include Humayun’s Tomb, Qutub Minar, Jama Masjid, Red Forts Lahori gate, India Gate, Digambar Jain Mandir with Gauri Shankar temple in the background, St. James’ Church, Hyderabad House and Lotus Temple . The city is also home to a vibrant culture and is known for its delicious food. In conclusion, Delhi is a city with a rich history and culture that offers much to see and experience. From its historic landmarks to its bustling streets and delicious food, Delhi is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in exploring Indias past and present.

Interesting Facts about Delhi

Here are some interesting facts about Delhi:
-The Red Fort was originally white: According to a survey conducted for archaeology, the Red Fort was made up of limestone and when the stone started to wither away, the British painted it red to preserve it.
-Delhi is home to the tallest minaret in the world: The Qutub Minar, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the tallest brick minaret in the world at 72.5 meters (237.8 ft).
-Delhi has Asia’s largest spice market: The Khari Baoli market, established in the 17th century, is known for its fantastic fragrance that attracts tourists and city dwellers alike.
-Delhi has Asia’s largest wholesale market for fruits and vegetables: The Azadpur Mandi is known for its vast variety of fruits and vegetables.
-Delhi has unique museums: One such museum is the Sulabh International Museum of Toilets which offers a perfect message on the history of sanitation.
-Delhi has the only Bahai Temple in Asia: The Lotus Temple, a Baháʼí House of Worship, is known for its beautiful architecture and serene atmosphere.
-Delhi has the most expensive office space in India: Connaught Place in New Delhi is known for its high-end office spaces.
-Delhi Metro platforms are user-friendly for blind people: The Delhi Metro has tactile paths and audio announcements to assist visually impaired passengers.
