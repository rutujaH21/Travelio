Introduction

Pune is a city located in the western Indian state of Maharashtra. Known as the “Queen of the Deccan,” Pune is the cultural capital of the Maratha people.
The city first gained importance as the capital of the Bhonsle Marathas in the 17th century.
It was temporarily captured by the Mughals but again served as the official Maratha capital from 1714 until its fall to the British in 1817

About Pune

Pune is a vibrant city with a rich history and culture. It is home to many historical landmarks such as Shaniwar Wada, Shinde Chhatri, and Vishrambaug Wada that date back to the Maratha Empire. The city is also known for its educational institutions and has been called the “Oxford of the East.” Pune has a thriving food scene with many restaurants serving traditional Maharashtrian cuisine as well as international fare. In recent years, Pune has emerged as a hub for information technology and manufacturing. The city has a bustling startup scene and is home to many multinational companies. Despite its rapid growth, Pune has managed to retain its charm and continues to attract visitors with its rich history, culture, and natural beauty.
